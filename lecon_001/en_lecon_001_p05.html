French?
Attends ! comes from the verb attendre — to wait. It's one of those words that shows up constantly in natural French, in exactly the same way English speakers blurt out "Wait!" when something surprises them. You'll hear it in conversation all the time.
Tu parles français ? is the first real sentence of the course — subject, verb, object. Notice that in spoken French, a question this natural doesn't need to be reordered the way English does ("Do you speak…?"). The words stay in the same order as a statement; only the tone rises. Tu parles français. → Tu parles français ? Same words, different music.
Français with a lowercase f means the language. With an uppercase F, Français means a French person. Same word — the capital letter does all the work.
