A different voice — again. This time it's not Ginette calling from off-camera — it's Bek and Keb themselves, stepping out of the game for a second to answer their mom. Notice the handwriting-style lettering: that's their voice now, not Nagar's or Zyeu-Bleu's. Maman ! is simply how French-speaking kids call out to their mother — warm and informal. And d'accord is one of the most useful little words in French: it means okay, alright, agreed — you'll hear it everywhere.
-er verbs (continued): il/elle and on

il / elle parle arrive
on parle arrive
Back on page 6, you met je parle — "I speak". Here's the rest of the pattern: il, elle, and on all take the exact same ending as je — they sound identical. So il/elle parle sounds just like je parle, and the same goes for arriver: il/elle arrive, on arrive — same sound, same spelling.

About on: in everyday spoken French, on is the word people actually reach for to say "we" — far more often than the more formal nous. The convenient part? on conjugates exactly like il or elle, third person singular. So when Keb says on arrive, it's built and pronounced exactly like il arrive — it just means "we're coming" instead of "he's coming".
