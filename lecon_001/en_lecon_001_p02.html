Pan pan pan,
pin pin pin,
pon pon pon,
pun pun pun !
— Pan pan pan, pin pin pin, pon pon pon, pun pun pun !
Now it's Zyeu-Bleu's turn! He's not saying anything meaningful either — just humming back at Nagar in the same playful spirit, matching her energy sound for sound.
(Notice the four vowel sounds: pan, pin, pon, pun — each one is a different French nasal vowel.)
Tip — nasal vowels: French has sounds that don't quite exist in English, but you're closer than you think! You already use nasal sounds in words like song or ring. In French, on (as in pon) sounds like the English "ong" — but without finishing the "g". Just let the sound trail off through your nose before the "g" ever arrives. Same idea for an, in, and un — each is a vowel sound that hums gently through the nose.
