Fafafa, féféfé,
fififi, fofofo,
foufoufou, fufufu !
— Fafafa, féféfé, fififi, fofofo, foufoufou, fufufu !
Nagar isn't really saying anything here — she's humming a little made-up tune, just playing with sounds. No translation needed for this one!
(If you want to hum along: fa-fa-fa, feh-feh-feh, fee-fee-fee, fo-fo-fo, foo-foo-foo... and then fufufu!)
Tip for that last sound (the French "u"): notice how "foufoufou" feels — lips rounded, like the English "oo" in "food". Now, keeping your lips in that exact same rounded, kissing-like shape, try to say "ee" instead. That's it — that's the French u in "fufufu"! It's the same sound as the German "ü".
